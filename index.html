Curriculum & Pathway Design

Building a career-ready CS pathway at San Diego Unified

I designed a comprehensive CTE pathway aligned to both CTE Model Curriculum and CSTA standards. But the goal was never just standards alignment. I wanted every student who completed the pathway to leave with something real: at least one professional certification, a digital portfolio they were proud of, and genuine work-based learning experience. I built in equitable grading practices from the start and intentionally designed inclusive spaces that increased participation from female students, students with IEPs, and students from historically underrepresented communities.

Pathway completers leave with industry-recognized credentials, a professional digital portfolio, and hands-on WBL experience — not just a transcript.

AI Literacy & Industry Alignment

Redesigning curriculum with industry partners and building an AI-assisted agile framework

I partnered with ICT industry professionals to audit and redesign my curriculum so it reflects how real teams actually work — not how textbooks say they should. Out of that process came something I'm especially proud of: an AI-assisted agile framework for user-centered design. Students plan sprint goals, create documentation, conduct user testing and feedback cycles, and reflect on their progress throughout. It's process-based learning by design, which also means it's also AI-proof assessment! The product matters less than the thinking behind it.

Students experience the full design cycle — planning, building, testing, reflecting — using the same collaborative tools and practices they'll encounter in the workforce.

Career Readiness Resources

Giving every student the tools to land the job

I built a comprehensive career development resource folder available to any CTE student. It includes prompting guides for developing a professional brand, tailoring a resume, and building a digital portfolio. Every resource was developed in partnership with ICT industry mentors to make sure the practices actually reflect what hiring managers look for. The goal is simple: no student should walk out of a pathway program without knowing how to present themselves professionally.

A freely accessible, industry-validated toolkit that any CCTE educator can adopt to help students show up career-ready from day one of their job search.

Teaching & Leadership Philosophy

How I think about education in the age of AI

AI literacy is an equity issue, not a tech issue.

Every student deserves access to AI tools and the skills to use them responsibly, critically, and effectively. When we treat AI literacy as optional or advanced, we recreate the same gaps we've always had — just with newer technology. The students who need these skills most are the ones we can least afford to leave behind.

Assess the process, not the product.

In the age of AI, the product proves nothing while the thinking behind it proves everything. Process-based learning is AI-proof by design: when students plan, document, test, reflect, and iterate, we see their minds at work in ways no generated output can fake. This isn't just good assessment practice — it's the foundation of how real teams actually build things.

Educators can't lead students into a future they haven't entered themselves.

We cannot keep teaching from the sideline of a transformation that is already happening in our classrooms. Leading in the age of AI requires vulnerability — the willingness to learn alongside students, rebuild what instruction looks like, and collaborate with colleagues rather than wait for a directive from above. The educators who will move their schools forward are the ones brave enough to go first.
